Series Title: The Walking Dead
Genre: Drama, Horror, Thriller, Post-Apocalyptic
Year: 2010 - 2022
Creator: Frank Darabont (based on the comic series by Robert Kirkman, Tony Moore, and Charlie Adlard)
Starring: Andrew Lincoln, Norman Reedus, Melissa McBride, Danai Gurira, Lauren Cohan

The Walking Dead is a critically acclaimed post-apocalyptic horror drama television series that premiered on AMC in 2010 and concluded in 2022. The series is based on the comic book series of the same name by Robert Kirkman, Tony Moore, and Charlie Adlard. Set in a world devastated by a zombie apocalypse, the narrative follows a group of survivors navigating the challenges of a new, brutal reality where the undead roam the earth and human threats can be just as deadly.

The story begins with Rick Grimes (Andrew Lincoln), a sheriff’s deputy who awakens from a coma to find the world overrun by walkers—zombie-like creatures that once were humans. As Rick searches for his family, he encounters other survivors, including Daryl Dixon (Norman Reedus), Carol Peletier (Melissa McBride), and Michonne (Danai Gurira), forming a close-knit group. Together, they must confront not only the relentless threat of walkers but also rival groups of survivors, each with their own philosophies and moralities.

The Walking Dead explores profound themes of survival, humanity, and the moral dilemmas faced in dire situations. With its intense character development and emotional depth, the series has captivated audiences worldwide, prompting discussions about the nature of civilization, loyalty, and the human spirit's resilience. Over its eleven-season run, The Walking Dead has become a cultural phenomenon, influencing numerous spin-offs and a dedicated fanbase.
